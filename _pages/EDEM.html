EDEM

EDEM (Europa Direct Encounter Mission) is a concept of a landing mission to Europa – one of the Jupiter’s icy moons.
It is believed that Europa may be a habitable world. This is possible due to the suggestion that a giant salt water ocean exists beneath the ice crust of Europa. The thickness of the ice crust is estimated in the range of 3…30 km.

There exist several concepts of the Europa ice penetration, mainly based on the drilling or ice-melting principles. On the contrary to these concepts EDEM is focused on a kinetic ice penetration. The mission concept is based on the application of an ultra-strong momentum exchange tether that is used for efficient deceleration of the Europa landing module and acceleration of the ice-penetration assets.

The long-lasting angular acceleration of the tethered spacecraft is provided by a pair of electric thrusters. The acceleration phase lasts during the interplanetary flight, while the energy is provided by solar panels and onboard radioisotope generator of the landing module.

The project is at its early phase. The preliminary concept with details on a flight scheme and spacecraft layout will be presented at IAC 2017 in Adelaide, Australia.
